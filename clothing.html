    Traditional Dress in Vietnam during Tet

    Most Vietnamese people wear new clothes to celebrate Tet, or the Lunar new year, in order to promote a fresh beginning to the year. Although Western-style outfits are more convenient for daily chores, the traditional tunic, or Ao dai, reappears each Tet. These tunics add to the festival’s formal atmosphere.
    During Tet, many Vietnamese people wear Ao dai to celebrate the New Year. The most popular outfit is the Ao Dai, which is elegant and colorful. It is often worn by women, men, and children during family gatherings and cultural events. Wearing traditional dress is a way to show respect for culture and to welcome a new year with joy and pride.
